# Pre-Decision Checklist

Use this checklist before any buy, sell, add, or trim action in a stock or portfolio context.

---

## BUSINESS / POSITION UNDERSTANDING
- [ ] I can explain how the company/ETF makes money in 3 sentences.
- [ ] I reviewed the latest annual and quarterly filings (10-K / 10-Q) or prospectus.
- [ ] I know the key revenue streams and where they come from.
- [ ] I understand the competitive moat or factor exposure.
- [ ] I reviewed the last 3-5 years of financial trends (revenue, margins, cash flow).
- [ ] I checked for red flags (accounting changes, insider selling, cash flow vs. earnings divergence).

## PORTFOLIO FIT
- [ ] I know this position's current weight in my total portfolio.
- [ ] This trade will not exceed my single-stock limit (5-10% of total portfolio).
- [ ] This trade will not push my sector allocation above 20-25% of equity.
- [ ] I am not adding to a sector already above my target.
- [ ] I understand the correlation to my existing holdings.
- [ ] I am not making this change because of recent price movement.

## TAX IMPACT
- [ ] I know whether gains would be short-term or long-term.
- [ ] I estimated the tax owed if selling.
- [ ] I checked for tax-loss harvesting opportunities.
- [ ] I considered the 30-day wash sale window if selling at a loss.
- [ ] I considered whether this asset belongs in a taxable or tax-advantaged account.

## RISK MODELING
- [ ] I ran a stress test to see drawdown impact on my total portfolio.
- [ ] I know what a 20%, 40%, and 60% drop would mean in dollar terms.
- [ ] I can tolerate that drawdown without selling emotionally.
- [ ] I reviewed liquidity: can I exit this position without moving the market?

## BEHAVIORAL CHECK
- [ ] If I didn't already own this, would I buy it today at current price?
- [ ] I am not chasing a price that went up recently.
- [ ] I am not panic-selling because of recent weakness.
- [ ] I have written down my exit criteria before entering.

## OPPORTUNITY COST
- [ ] I compared this to my next-best alternative (another stock, index fund, cash).
- [ ] I considered fees, taxes, and transaction costs.
- [ ] I considered whether I should pay down debt instead.

## DECISION RECORD
- [ ] I logged the reasoning, expected outcome, and exit criteria in my decision journal.
- [ ] I set a review date to reassess this decision.
